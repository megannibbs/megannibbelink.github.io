Megan Nibbelink

Hi there! Thanks for stopping by.

I'm a PhD student in the Ecology and Evolutionary Biology Department at the University of Kansas. My research interests include paleobotany, plant morphology & anatomy, systematic theory, and computational biology as they relate to living and extinct ferns and lycophytes.

Aside from my research, I am passionate advocate for equity and access in education. I am excited to begin working with KU's Center for Digital Inclusion soon on a project that aims to help women who have been involved with the justice system build computer literacy skills. Read more about the RETURN Project here.

I am currently working towards becoming a workshop instructor through The Carpentries, a nonprofit organization whose mission is to teach coding and data science skills to researchers.Read more about The Carpentries here.
