Teaching

In addition to my experience as a tutor, supplemental instruction leader, and teaching assistant, I have recently begun to pursue additional opportunities at KU to expand my teaching repertoire.

The Carpentries

Only 10-25% of professional positions in computing, software development, and bioinformatics are held by women. As I develop my own coding skills, one of my priorities is to make it easier for other women to exist in these spaces as well. I am currently finishing up my training to be an instructor for software and data carpentries workshops through The Carpentries - a nonprofit whose mission is to provide practical coding and data skills to researchers worldwide. As an instructor in this field, I hope to create a space that makes coding feel more accessible to women.

The RETURN Project

I am excited to begin working with KU's Center for Digital Inclusion on the RETURN project. In this role, I am currently working to develop the program's first introductory coding curriculum. This program is designed to aid women who have previously been involved with the justice system build computer literacy skills. Learn more about the RETURN Project.
